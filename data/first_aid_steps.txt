Title: First Aid Step-by-Step Instructions
Source: Curated first-aid reference (based on standard first-aid protocols)

Nosebleeds - Treatment Steps:
Sit upright and lean slightly forward, not backward, to avoid swallowing blood. Pinch the soft part of the nose (just below the bony bridge) firmly for 10-15 minutes continuously without releasing to check. Breathe through your mouth while doing this. Apply a cold compress to the bridge of the nose if available. Avoid tilting the head back, as this can cause blood to flow down the throat. Seek medical care if bleeding continues beyond 20 minutes, is very heavy, or occurs after a head injury.

Burns - Treatment Steps:
For minor burns, cool the affected area under cool (not ice-cold) running water for 10-20 minutes. Remove any tight items like rings near the burn before swelling starts. Do not apply ice, butter, or toothpaste, as these can worsen tissue damage. Cover loosely with a clean, non-stick, sterile dressing. Do not pop any blisters that form. Seek emergency care for burns larger than 3 inches, burns on the face, hands, or genitals, or burns that appear white or charred.

Choking (Conscious Adult) - Treatment Steps:
Ask the person if they can speak or cough. If they cannot speak, cough, or breathe, stand behind them and perform the Heimlich maneuver: place a fist just above the navel, grasp it with your other hand, and give quick, firm upward thrusts. Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unresponsive. If they become unresponsive, lower them to the ground and begin CPR, checking the mouth for the object before each set of rescue breaths. Call emergency services immediately if the obstruction does not clear.

Cuts and Wounds - Treatment Steps:
Clean the wound under running water for at least 5 minutes to remove dirt and debris. Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth or sterile gauze to stop bleeding. Once bleeding slows, apply an antiseptic ointment and cover with a sterile bandage. Change the dressing daily and keep the wound clean and dry. Seek medical attention if bleeding does not stop after 10 minutes of direct pressure, if the wound is deep or gaping, or if it was caused by a rusty or dirty object.

Sprains and Strains - Treatment Steps:
Follow the R.I.C.E. method: Rest the injured area and avoid putting weight on it. Ice the area for 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours for the first 48 hours, using a cloth barrier to protect the skin. Compress the area with an elastic bandage, wrapped snugly but not too tight. Elevate the limb above heart level when possible to reduce swelling. Seek medical attention if there is severe pain, visible deformity, or inability to bear weight or move the joint.

Fever - Management Steps:
For adults, a temperature above 100.4°F (38°C) is generally considered a fever. Rest and stay well hydrated with water or oral rehydration solutions. Over-the-counter antipyretics such as paracetamol or ibuprofen can help reduce fever, following package dosing instructions. Dress in lightweight clothing and keep the room at a comfortable temperature. Seek medical attention if fever exceeds 103°F (39.4°C), lasts more than 3 days, or is accompanied by a stiff neck, severe headache, difficulty breathing, or confusion.

Allergic Reactions - Treatment Steps:
For mild reactions such as localized rash or itching, an over-the-counter antihistamine can help relieve symptoms. Watch closely for signs of a severe reaction (anaphylaxis), including difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or throat, rapid pulse, dizziness, or hives spreading across the body. Anaphylaxis is a medical emergency: use an epinephrine auto-injector immediately if the person has one prescribed, and call emergency services right away even if symptoms seem to improve after the injection.
